West African Grilled Beef (Suya)

Aaah, In the words of an African proverb, "Good food is the foundation of genuine happiness," I present to you SUYA!

Prepare to embark on a culinary journey that captures the essence of African cuisine, where vibrant spices and tender beef come together in perfect harmony. As the grill sizzles and the aroma of the tantalizing marinade fills the air, you'll discover a true celebration of flavor that brings joy to every bite. But first, you need this;

INGREDIENTS

  • 1/4 cup peanut oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 2 tablespoons ground peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon ground cayenne pepper (adjust to taste for spiciness)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Skewers for grilling
  1. Soak the skewers in water for about 30 minutes to prevent them from burning during grilling.
  2. Soak the skewers in water for about 30 minutes to prevent them from burning during grilling.
  3. Thread the thinly sliced beef onto the skewers, making sure to leave some space between the pieces for even cooking.
  4. Generously brush the beef skewers with the spice paste, making sure to coat all sides. Allow them to marinate for at least 30 minutes, or refrigerate for a few hours to let the flavors meld.
  5. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  6. Place the marinated beef skewers on the grill and cook for about 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the beef is nicely charred and cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  7. Remove the skewers from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before serving.
  8. Serve the West African Grilled Beef (Suya) hot, garnished with additional ground peanuts and accompanied by sliced onions, tomatoes, and peppers if desired.

Suya is traditionally served as a street food snack or as part of a larger meal, and it pairs well with a side of jollof rice, roasted plantains, or a refreshing salad. Let the lingering taste of vibrant spices and tender beef leave you craving for more. Remember, the only beef you'll have is deciding who gets the last skewer!
